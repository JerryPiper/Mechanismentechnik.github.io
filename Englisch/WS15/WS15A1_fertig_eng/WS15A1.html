1. the degree of freedom for the whole system

  • In the first part of the task is asked for the total degree of freedom FFF. This is calculated according to Grübler with the following formula:
F=3 (n−1)−b1−2 b2F=3\,(n-1)-b_1-2\,b_2F=3(n−1)−b1​−2b2​
  • nnn is the number of links, b1b_{1}b1​ is the number of the monovalent and b2b_{2}b2​ is the number for the divalent joints.
  • The number of links is composed of 0, 1, 2, 3 and 4. The first link is represented as a surface because it has more than two link joints.
  • The movable bearing at the top is a monovalent joint and the only one in this mechanism.
  • For the bivalent joints we have a number of b2=5b_{2}=5b2​=5. For that the fixed bearing on the right side and the connection between the thrust crank (4) and the environment are counted.

  • The remaining three joints are the link joints between 4 → 2, 2 → 1, and 1 → 3.
  • Our result would be:
F=3 (5−1)−1−2⋅5=1F=3\,(5-1)-1-2\cdot5 = 1F=3(5−1)−1−2⋅5=1
