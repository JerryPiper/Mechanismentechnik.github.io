Two-layer synthesis

The frame point A0A_0A0​ and the link point BBB of a four-bar linkage must be determined for the two end positions of the illustrated cover.

The link points have to be on the straight line aaa and the frame points on bbb.

Draw the complete four-bar linkage in position 1.

Solution:

  • First, connect the end points of the two lids (D1→D2D_1 \rightarrow D_2D1​→D2​) with a straight line and draw the first perpendicular bisector.
  • Our point P is the intersection of the two perpendicular bisectors, around which we will turn later to find the points which are asked in the exercise.

Procedure for determining link point BBB:

  • First we determine the pole angle φ0\varphi_0φ0​.
  • This is located between the leg sa\color {#5858FA} s_asa​, which passes through the points PPP and C1C1C1, and the center line ma\color {#5858FA} m_ama​, which also equal the perpendicular bisector of the line C1C_1C1​ to C2C_2C2​.

  • Now we rotate the two lines with the angle φ0\varphi_0φ0​ around the point P until our midline ma\color {#5858FA} m_ama​ intersects our frame point B0B_0B0​.
  • Since in the task is given that our link point BBB must be somewhere on the line aaa, so it is the intersection of the leg sa\color {#5858FA} s_asa​ and the line aaa.
